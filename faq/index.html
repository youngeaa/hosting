Pricing & Funding

Ramp cost depends on the height of your entry, the length of ramp needed, and any landings or turns required. Every project is different, which is why we do a free home assessment before quoting anything.

We'll give you a clear, itemized quote with no hidden fees after we see your home.

Standard homeowner's insurance typically does not cover accessibility modifications unless they're needed due to a covered loss (like an accident). However, some policies have riders or endorsements that may help.

We recommend calling your insurance provider directly to ask about coverage for accessibility renovations before your assessment.

Yes — several programs may be available depending on your situation:

  • USDA Rural Development offers home repair loans and grants for low-income rural residents
  • Louisiana Medicaid HCBS waivers may cover modifications for eligible individuals
  • VA grants (SAH and SHA) are available for qualifying veterans with service-connected disabilities
  • Local Area Agencies on Aging sometimes provide funding or referrals for seniors

We're happy to discuss your options during the free assessment.

Installation

It depends on the modification:

  • Grab bars: typically 1–2 hours
  • Wheelchair ramp: usually 1–2 days depending on size
  • Doorway widening: 1–2 days per doorway
  • Roll-in shower conversion: 3–5 days depending on scope

We'll give you a realistic timeline during the assessment so you know exactly what to expect.

Permit requirements vary by parish and municipality in Louisiana. Grab bars typically don't require a permit. Ramps and structural modifications like doorway widening may require one depending on your location.

We handle all permit research and filing as part of the project — you don't need to figure that out on your own.

For most jobs — grab bars, ramps, and similar work — you can stay home the entire time. For larger renovations like shower conversions or doorway widening, you may need to avoid the work area during certain phases, but you generally don't need to vacate your home.

We'll let you know upfront if anything requires you to make other arrangements.

We stand behind our work. If anything isn't right after we leave, call us and we'll come back to make it right — no hassle, no runaround. Our goal is for you to feel confident and safe in your home long after installation day.
